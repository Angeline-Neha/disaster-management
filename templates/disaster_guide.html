Drop, Cover & Hold

Before an Earthquake:

  • Pack an emergency kit
  • Secure heavy furniture
  • Create a family communication and evacuation plan
  • Practice “Drop, Cover, and Hold On” drills

During an Earthquake:

  • Indoors: Drop, cover, and hold on
  • Outdoors: Move to an open area away from hazards
  • In a vehicle: Stop and stay inside until shaking stops

After an Earthquake:

  • Check for injuries and structural damage
  • Watch for hazards like gas leaks and fires
  • Be prepared for aftershocks
  • Stay informed via emergency alerts

Evacuation Ready

Before a Cyclone:

  • Pack an emergency kit with food, water, and documents
  • Secure your home and outdoor objects
  • Trim trees and fix loose roof parts
  • Know your evacuation routes and nearest shelter
  • Monitor weather updates from official sources

During a Cyclone:

  • Stay indoors, away from windows and doors
  • Turn off gas and unplug appliances
  • Do not go outside until authorities declare it's safe

After a Cyclone:

  • Wait for the official all-clear before going outside
  • Watch out for fallen power lines and floodwaters
  • Check on neighbors and help where possible
  • Report damage and follow local authority instructions

Create Defensible Space

Before a Wildfire:

  • Create a defensible space by clearing flammable vegetation near your home
  • Prepare an emergency kit and evacuation plan
  • Keep important documents and valuables ready to go
  • Monitor local fire alerts and warnings

During a Wildfire:

  • Evacuate immediately if instructed by authorities
  • Wear protective clothing (long sleeves, mask)
  • Keep windows and doors closed to reduce smoke exposure
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts for updates

After a Wildfire:

  • Return only when officials say it’s safe
  • Watch for hotspots and smoldering debris
  • Avoid damaged structures until inspected
  • Document losses for insurance claims

Stay Above Water

Before a Flood:

  • Know your flood risk and evacuation routes
  • Prepare an emergency kit and store valuables on higher ground
  • Install check valves in plumbing to prevent backups
  • Keep sandbags ready if in a flood-prone area

During a Flood:

  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Do not walk or drive through floodwaters
  • Disconnect electrical appliances if safe to do so
  • Listen to weather updates and emergency instructions

After a Flood:

  • Avoid floodwaters, which may be contaminated or electrically charged
  • Do not enter buildings until they are declared safe
  • Clean and disinfect everything that got wet
